    ‘The Orator’ from NSBM

    NSBM students swept the board at “The Orator”: the speech competition organized by NIBM. Amidst contestants that represented NSBM and island wide centres of NIBM 5 students from NSBM were among the 12 finalists at the final round held on 25th June at NIBM Auditorium.



    The competition was held in 3 rounds evaluated by renowned experts in Public Speaking while the final round was held in the presence of the Management of NIBM & NSBM, students and staff from both institutions and parents. All the competitors were trained by a specialist in Public Speaking which resulted in high competition at the final round. The finalists, with exceptional oratorical skills, were vying for ‘The Orator 2014” award of which the winner was Nuwan Tharanga Rajapakshe, of NSBM. Ruckshani Weerasekara became the Second Runner Up while Naduni Jayasinghe and Sumalka Mendis secured 4th and 5th places respectively.

    Progress Review and Media Brief on Green University Town

    A media briefing and a progress review of the construction of NSBM Green University town, a venture inspired by the vision of Mahinda Chintanaya to develop Sri Lanka to be the ‘Knowledge Hub’ of the Asian region, was held on the 27th of May at 11.30 a.m. at the site of Green University Town in Pitipana, Homagama, presided by Hon. Dullas Alahapperuma, Minister of Youth Affaires and Skills Development.

    Construction site of the Green University Town was inspected by Hon. Dullas Alahapperuma and the progress report of the project was handed over to Hon. Minister by Dr. E.A. Weerasinghe, CEO, NSBM. During the program, journalists and other media representatives who witnessed the progress of the Green University Project were further informed about it.



    The Green University Town project which commenced construction in Pitipana, Homagama in a 26- acre land in December 2013, is a Rs. 10.2 billion investment. Instigating the ‘Mahinda Chinthana’ to make Sri Lanka the ‘Knowledge Hub’ of the region, constructions were inaugurated in November 2013; taking the Sri Lankan higher education experience into unprecedented heights. The construction of this immense project that exceeds the total building area of 80000 m2 is being efficiently managed by currently employing 2000 workers so as to reach its completion in December 2015.

    Introduction to Android Development (NSBM DevOps)

    Mobile development can be considered as a trending area within developers around the globe. Learning about mobile development in relation to computing by any undergraduate can be considered a timely necessity.


    The largest share of mobile development around the globe was owned by Android Operating System. Android Operating System is also the most popular and trending mobile Operating System in the globe. It has been primarily designed for touch screens mobile devices such as smartphones and tablets. Its an open source and thousands of people are involved in Android community for development. Since its popularity grows on, there has to be more Android Developers to satisfy the android users with good applications.



    Taking this scenario to the real world, DevOps Community of National School of Business Management organized an event on Mobile Development based on the theme of “Introduction to Android Development” in collaboration with Exilesoft (Pvt) Limited . The main target audience of this workshop was the NSBM undergraduates who are amature to android development by letting them to boost their skills on this area and bring it to the next level. The resource person for the workshop was Mr. Sanath Nandasiri from Exilesoft. He vividly explained the audience in designing an Android app from scratch by designing a Notepad application in real time. He also clarified many vital points to consider as an Android appllication developer and provided substantial answers to the questions raised by the audience on Android development.


    After organizing many successful workshops such as “Towards Agile” and “The Apprentice” this event was also turned out to be an another successful one with the participation of more than 100 students. The “DevOps” Community would like to express their gratitude to academic and non-academic staff of National School of Business Management, the students and all the participants who had shown their valuable support to make this workshop a success. Special thanks goes to Mr.Sanath for making the workshop more significant event.

    NSBM Software Competition 2014

    Believing that “Competition is the driving force for innovation”, National School of Business Management launched the NSBM Software Competition - NSC 2014. This year the competition open for NSBM students in teams of 4 to 6. Students were encouraged to form groups with multidisciplinary backgrounds. The NSC seeks to explore ability of graphic designing, usability designing, web development and software engineering aspects of NSBM student population. Participating students are provided with special training programs to sharpen their knowledge and skills. NSBM expects to grow NSC to national level in forth coming years.
